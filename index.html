Explore the Myth

Introduction

Dodékathlos is an innovative board game that seamlessly integrates physical components with digital tools. This project was developed as the final examination for the Digital Heritage and Multimedia course, within the Master's Degree in Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge (DHDK) at the University of Bologna.

Inspired by the legendary Twelve Labors of Hercules, the game is uniquely designed to serve as both an engaging and educational activity tailored for the Archaeological Museum of Olympia. Before crafting the core gameplay and interaction mechanics, our team conducted an in-depth museum analysis to align the experience with the venue's rich heritage.

Preliminary Investigation

The Museum

The Archaeological Museum of Olympia houses one of the world's most prestigious collections of ancient Greek artifacts, spanning from prehistoric times to the Roman era. The location includes the archeological site that was discovered in 1766 by the British traveler Richard Chandler and brought back to life thanks to the German Archeological Institute starting from 1875. The exhibition of the museum collects the manufacts that have been discovered in the excavations nearby in order to narrate the story of the Sanctuary of Zeus from prehistoric times to the early Christian centuries. Celebrated for its extraordinary masterpieces, the museum showcases iconic works offering visitors an unparalleled journey through classical antiquity.

Of particular significance to our project are the monumental remnants of the sculpted metopes from the ancient Temple of Zeus. These masterpieces vividly depict the Twelve Labors of Hercules. Interestingly, the architectural structure of this temple is believed to be the historical reason why the canon of Hercules' exploits was fixed at exactly twelve, as Greek mythology actually records numerous other heroic deeds performed by the demigod.

Metopes of the Temple of Zeus (Apples of the Hesperides and the Cretan Bull)

The star assets of the museum are the fame that it has and the importance of the exhibition itself for the History of Art and History in general. In fact, this institution is well-known worldwide and it does not seem to need more publicity: visitors and tourists come from all over the world, attracted by the sculptures, the excavations, the old stadium, and the olive tree that anyone into sports recognizes as the place where the Olympic Flame is lit up still now.

The institutional goals that we have detected involve more interaction with visitors and to enlarge the number of proposals of educational activities. Despite being famous and important, the visit is currently confined to a "look and move on" dynamic: visitors cannot do anything more than observe the artifacts and the corresponding labels. Connected to this, while there are some activities for school groups, their primary audience consists of elementary school children, whereas our project is specifically designed for slightly older middle school teenagers.

The Audience

The primary audience chosen consists of students aged 11 and over who visit the exhibition with their families or with their school groups. Our main goals are to entertain them in a setting that could otherwise feel stagnant, and to promote social interaction, reinforcing relationships with their classmates or simply sharing a meaningful moment with their relatives.

However, this audience can face significant psychological barriers, such as low self-esteem, or they might initially find the museum's traditional display irrelevant to their daily lives. This is a critical age group, and our project aims to break these friction points to raise genuine interest.

The secondary audience that we hope will choose to play our game is whoever is interested in Greek mythology, Archeology or History, so they will be motivated by personal relevance and academic interest. This project offers digital insights of the museum, in order to add something to what enthusiasts may have already learned.

Despite our good intentions they could still find this game irrelevant because for them it is too simple or childish. Another point is that it has a cost too high and they cannot afford it: visiting the museum could be a costly expense for someone, e.g. a family or those who come from afar, so adding also this extra could be an obstacle.

For this audience, the goals consist of feedback between visitors and environment and stimulate emotional responses: the game should add something to the plain visit and make good memories about the museum while interacting with the environment and the others.

For both audiences there are no special capabilities to learn: computer softwares, gaming mechanics and mobile apps are sufficient. They both are able to handle devices like computers, tablets and smartphones.

Related Works

Before developing our project, we conducted extensive market research on existing games centered around Hercules and Greek mythology. Our goal was to understand the state of the art and pinpoint what unique value our game could bring to the table. During this phase, we analyzed several prominent titles, including Elysium, Mythomakya, Micro Heroes: Hercules, and Hercules and the 12 Labours.

Although Greek mythology serves as a foundational theme in many modern games, we noticed that very few actually feature Hercules as their main protagonist. Furthermore, almost none of them delve into the depth of his original myth; instead, Hercules is often treated merely as a generic, strong hero. Most importantly, the archaeological aspect and material sources are completely discarded. Our game is specifically designed to bridge this gap, integrating tangible historical education directly into the core gameplay loop.

To fine-tune our system, we also closely evaluated the gameplay mechanics of legendary titles we were already familiar with, such as Cluedo and Dungeons & Dragons. This deep dive allowed us to draw valuable inspiration for our investigation elements and role-playing layers, blending proven mechanics into a completely new, educational format.

The Game

The Game Mechanics

Dodékathlos is a hybrid cooperative board game designed for players aged 11 and above, accommodating groups of up to five people. It seamlessly combines traditional physical board game components—such as a board, dice, and cards—with a dedicated mobile application. The companion app is mandatory, acting as an interactive guide that properly orchestrates the narrative and mechanics throughout the entire experience.

To succeed, players must work closely together to complete the legendary mythological Labours of Hercules. Participants can choose from five distinct characters: Hercules (which is mandatory in single-player mode), Iolaus, Athena, Theseus, or Hermes. Each character possesses unique special abilities known as Core Attributes that are crucial for achieving victory. Every single Labour features a specific Difficulty Class and requires a combination of three distinct Core Attributes to be successfully overcome.

The game offers two distinct game modes tailored to the players' preferences: the Short Modality (requiring six tasks to complete) and the Long Modality (requiring all twelve tasks). The ultimate objective is to successfully secure at least four Labours of Hercules in the short mode, or at least eight Labours when tackling the long mode.

The Nike of Paionios has been chosen as the interactive guide of the game due to its immense historical significance within the museum setup. As one of the absolute jewels in the museum's crown, this statue stands directly in front of visitors as they walk into the building, occupying the exact center of the room where the metopes are collected, creating a direct physical and narrative link between the board game and the actual archaeological space.

The gameplay revolves around five Core Attributes: Strength, Dexterity, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma. To beat a Labour's Difficulty Class (CD), players must choose their characters strategically and combine their static attribute points with the physical dice rolls, working together to overcome the challenges set by the gods.

Time is a restricted resource managed directly by the application. Players start their epic journey with a pool of twelve Hourglasses in Short Mode and twenty-six in Long Mode. Simply entering and attempting a new Labour costs one Hourglass. If a dice roll fails, players can spend one additional Hourglass to retry immediately. However, a Labour can be attempted a maximum of three times (costing a total of three Hourglasses) before it results in automatic defeat and forces a retreat.

To encourage real-world exploration, visiting the Archaeological Museum of Olympia and scanning the physical QR codes hidden near the exhibits will permanently unlock Special Perks assigned to the character cards. These advantages act as persistent gameplay tools designed to facilitate victory in all future matches at home.

The game features custom physical dice where the standard number 1 is replaced by a divine Lightning Bolt symbol. If this symbol is rolled during a challenge, the player is immediately forced to draw a card from the Peril & Aid Deck.

This unpredictable deck contains powerful Zeus's Aid cards, which can grant crucial bonuses to help you win the Labour, as well as disruptive Hera's Interference cards, which can decrease the value of your dice score, threatening to cause an immediate failure.

The Gameplay Loop

To begin the adventure, players open the physical board and initialize the companion mobile application. They input the number of participants, select their preferred game length (Short or Long Modality), and choose their mythological characters. The Nike of Paionios then introduces the myth of Hercules to set the stage.

When resolving a Labour, the player currently in charge spends one Hourglass via the app interface and rolls the physical dice. In case of a successful roll, players tap "Victory" in the app and place a Gold Coin on the corresponding spot on the board. Nike will then narrate how Hercules overcame that specific trial in mythology, displaying the actual archaeological metope from the Temple of Zeus. Conversely, if players fail the challenge three consecutive times, they must tap "Defeat" in the app, place a Black Coin on the board, and move forward to the next task. Regardless of the final outcome—victory or complete defeat—Nike concludes the match by announcing that the journey continues in the real world, motivating players to explore the physical museum to find hidden QR codes.

Phygital Interaction & Museum Integration

The core interaction of the project is engineered around this seamless phygital mobile ecosystem. The app does not merely act as a passive rulebook, but rather breathes life into the Nike of Paionios as a dynamic in-game narrator, guiding players, managing resources, and offering insightful explanations of both the myths and the actual historical metopes.

To bridge the gap between home entertainment and cultural heritage, physical QR codes are strategically placed near the metopes inside the museum galleries. This layout serves as a real-world expansion of the tabletop experience, directly triggering the unlockable Special Perks on the physical hero cards and turning the museum visit into an active, gamified journey.

User Journey

Discovery: The game will be promoted in the museum’s shop and on the museum’s social media channels for reinforcing the visitor-institution relation. It positions the museum not just as a static building, but as an active, modern institution. The game introduces remote players to the Archaeological Museum of Olympia for the first time, sparking curiosity.

Onboarding: Players realize they can choose their environment: they can play directly inside the museum, outdoors in the surrounding park, or back at home. The app is intuitive and immediately immerses players in the game, smoothly guiding them through the fundamental scoring mechanics and the Hourglass mechanic.

Capture/Interaction: The Nike that comes to life is a nice surprise for the players because it is rare seeing a statue that speaks and moves. The app is easy to use and users will find no difficulties in understanding its operation, especially for the mechanics of time (the Hourglasses) and scoring.

After experience: Win or lose, players finish the session feeling satisfied but craving more. Nike announces that the adventure continues in the real world. This creates a powerful incentive for players to visit (or return to) the Archaeological Museum of Olympia to scan QR codes and permanently unlock character perks for their next playthrough at home.

Conclusion & Further Development

Dodékathlos is a project that aims to help the Archaeological Museum of Olympia in those aspects where something is missing. The game will contribute to modernizing the museum and improving the visit toward an involving relationship between the environment and the visitors. Apart from that, both an educational and a social aspect are touched: players will learn something more specific about archaeological discoveries and Greek myths. Anyone will be involved in the game with a specific role, so nobody will be excluded and everyone will bring a special contribution in order to win.

Limitations of the project rely on the fact that it will be promoted only by the museum’s channels, so maybe it would not reach a big audience worldwide. In addition to that, those already well-versed in the subject matter may find the game lacking in depth and, consequently, boring.

In conclusion, we would like to emphasize that this is only a basic version of the game; if successful, we are committed to introducing new expansions to keep the experience fresh and original. The team intends, for example, to focus future efforts on incorporating other works of art from the museum into the game or delving deeper into the figure of Hercules. Another future goal is the integration of new technologies —such as screens or gaming stations— within the building, to enrich the on-site experience.
